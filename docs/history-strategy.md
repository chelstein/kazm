# KAZM History Strategy

## KAZM 106.5 FM & 780 AM

## Mellow Mountain Radio

### Purpose

This document defines how KAZM preserves and presents its history.

It governs:

* Station history
* Community history
* Broadcast history
* Technical history
* Local memories

KAZM has broadcast in Sedona since November 1, 1974. That longevity is a strategic asset. The goal of this strategy is to turn decades of broadcasting into a durable, museum-quality archive that serves listeners, honors the community, and grows more valuable every year.

History at KAZM is not a single About page. It is an ongoing preservation program.

The governing rule is simple: nothing enters the archive unless it is true and sourced, or clearly labeled as unverified. Accuracy is the product.

---

# Guiding Principles

* History is preservation, not promotion.
* The community is a co-author of the record.
* Verified facts come first; remembered history is honored but labeled.
* Gaps are disclosed, never filled with invention.
* The archive is built to outlast everyone working on it today.

For the editorial standards that apply to writing and reviewing historical content, see the `history` skill in `.claude/skills/history/SKILL.md`. This document covers the archive itself: what we collect, how we describe it, and how the community contributes.

# Historical Content Categories

The archive is organized into the following categories. Each item belongs to at least one.

## Station Timeline

A chronological record of the station's life, anchored by canonical milestones:

* Original sign-on: November 1, 1974
* Ownership transition to Cutter Grind Broadcasting LLC initiated in 2023

Every entry carries a date (or a clearly marked date range) and a source.

## Historic Photos

Images of the station, staff, facilities, equipment, events, and the community. Cataloged with date, location, people, and photographer when known.

## Historic Audio

Airchecks, recorded programs, station IDs, interviews, and event coverage. Preserved in original form and presented with context.

## Historic Advertisements

Local advertising as a record of the regional economy and community life over time — spots, scripts, print materials, and sponsor history.

## Historic Programming

Past shows, formats, schedules, and special broadcasts, including the station's evolution toward its current Full Service format and Mellow Mountain Radio brand.

## Community Events

The station's presence at and coverage of community life in Sedona, the Verde Valley, and Northern Arizona.

## Oral Histories

First-person recollections from listeners, former staff, and community members, preserved as testimony with the speaker's name, the recording date, and context.

## Ownership History

A documented, chronological record of station ownership, anchored on the 2023 transition to Cutter Grind Broadcasting LLC, with prior ownership recorded as sources allow.

## Engineering History

Technical lineage of the station: frequency (780 kHz AM), translator (106.5 FM K293DA), FCC Facility ID (64494), transmitter sites, power, antennas, and studio history.

## Sports Broadcast History

The station's history of carrying regional and Arizona sports, including Arizona Cardinals, Arizona Diamondbacks, Phoenix Suns, Arizona State Athletics, and Northern Arizona University Athletics.

# Preservation Standards

These standards apply to every item in every category.

## Metadata

Each archive item records, at minimum:

* Title or short description
* Category
* Date or date range
* People involved
* Location
* Contributor and source
* Rights / permission status
* Verification status (Verified, Oral History, Unverified, Source Needed)

Unknown fields are marked "unknown" — never invented.

## Dates

* Use full dates whenever known (for example, November 1, 1974).
* Mark approximate dates explicitly (for example, "circa 1978" or "year unconfirmed").
* Never silently approximate or round a date to make a timeline look complete.

## Source Verification

* Prefer primary sources: FCC records, station documents, original recordings, photographs, and dated print materials.
* Corroborate remembered history against documentation where possible.
* Record what each claim rests on so a future archivist can re-check it.
* When a claim cannot be verified, label it Unverified or Source Needed rather than dropping or asserting it.

## Attribution

* Credit photographers, recorders, contributors, and rights holders.
* Preserve the names of people in oral histories and the context in which they spoke.
* Never present a paraphrase as a direct quote.

## Archiving

* Preserve originals; present derivatives (web-optimized images, clipped audio).
* Keep originals and metadata together so context is never lost.
* Store the archive so it survives staff changes, redesigns, and platform changes.
* Treat the archive as permanent infrastructure, not website content to be discarded at the next refresh.

# Community Archive Program

KAZM's history belongs to the community as much as to the station. Much of it lives in shoeboxes, garages, and memories rather than in any official file. The Community Archive Program invites listeners to help preserve it.

We welcome:

* **Listener submissions** — stories and memories of KAZM and the region
* **Historic photos** — of the station, staff, events, equipment, and community life
* **Airchecks** — recordings of past KAZM broadcasts, however informal
* **Station memorabilia** — bumper stickers, print ads, schedules, swag, documents
* **Community stories** — the moments KAZM was part of, big and small

How contributions are handled:

* Every contribution is cataloged with its contributor, date, and context.
* Contributors are credited, and rights and permissions are confirmed before anything is published.
* Originals are protected; the community keeps its property unless it chooses to donate.
* Submissions are reviewed for accuracy and clearly labeled by verification status before entering the public archive.

Each contribution helps fill a gap in the record. Where the archive is incomplete, that is an open invitation — not a finished story.

# The Standard

KAZM is more than a radio station. KAZM is part of the story of Sedona and Northern Arizona.

This strategy exists to make sure that story is preserved accurately, attributed honestly, and carried forward for the listeners who come after us.

If an item cannot meet that standard, it is verified, labeled, or held — not published.
